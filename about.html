West Coast Education, Texas Roots About My Journey

I'm a senior at UC Berkeley studying data science who was raised in Texas. I'm a National Merit Scholar, and I graduated as Valedictorian of the Class of 2017 at Lamar High School. As a student, I choose to explore data science as it relates to the intersection of linguistics and computer science. I am dedicated to being an ethical programmer and building technology that helps people.

I have had the honor to conduct research in several university labs. At UT Austin, I worked as a research assistant under Dr. Juliana Duncan to computationally simulate chemical reactions for fuel cells. Later on, in my sophomore year at UC Berkeley, I helped to lay the groundwork for building machine learning models for climate chemistry datasets with the Cohen Research Group.

Now I am a coder who has developed a profound interest in languages. Apart from Python, Java, and SQL, I am currently working to become fluent in Japanese and improve my Cantonese, Mandarin, and Latin.

In my free time, I enjoy making music on the piano, watching anime, and playing tennis & soccer with friends. I am very thankful for the things I can still enjoy while in quarantine.
