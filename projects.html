Machine Learning Mapping NO2 datasets

In order to accurately measure the NO2 pollutant levels in our atmosphere, the Cohen Group created the (BEHR) Berkeley High-Resolution NO2 Product to generate high-resolution NO2 measurements that corrected for spatial and seasonal variations in NO2 levels recorded by the OMI instrument onboard NASA's Aura satellite. In an effort to build on their work, I began work on a Machine Learning model that might be used to replace the computationally expensive WRF-Chem climate model that produces these measurements. In my work with the Cohen Group, I successfully aligned 47K+ WRF-Chem pixels with OMI pixels to enable better data correlation during training for an ML model.

Natural Language Processing Neural Sequence Labeling

Part-of-speech tagging is the process of marking the part of speech for words, which can be performed by creating a Neural Sequence Labeling model. POS tagging is considered an integral part of NLP, in order to utilize the syntax of languages to better understand language semantics (meaning). I built a POS-tagger model by implementing an LSTM and using mini-batch stochastic gradient descent on pre-trained GloVe embeddings. I improved model accuracy by implementing a bi-directional LSTM and dropout layer, and loading the 500,000 most common 300-dimensional word embeddings.

Sociolinguistic Variation Study at UC Berkeley Analyzing Variation in Confirmation and Declination responses

I explored the sociolinguistic variation in "yes" and "no" response variants for English speakers at UC Berkeley. By collecting auditory linguistic data over the span of two weeks, I looked for significant correlations between variant usage and social factors of gender, race, formality of relationship, and situational context.
