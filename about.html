West Coast Education, Texas Roots About My Journey

As a data-driven individual with a passion for leveraging data into valuable insights, I have had tremendous academic aspirations. I earned my B.A. in Data Science at UC Berkeley as a National Merit Scholar, and I graduated as Valedictorian of Lamar High School. Throughout my UC Berkeley career, I delved into the intersection of data science with linguistics and computer science. I am finishing my M.S. in Computer Science at UT Austin, focusing on Deep Learning and Machine learning.

I have had the great honor of conducting research in several university labs. At UT Austin, I worked as a researcher under Dr. Juliana Duncan to computationally simulate chemical reactions for fuel cells. AT UC Berkeley, I helped lay the groundwork for building an ML model for climate chemistry datasets with the Cohen Research Group. Working on large-scale projects with varying types of data has enabled me to become adaptable and familiar with data analysis techniques.

I worked as a software engineer at Smarsh, integrating many new features into their surveillance application for major financial institutions. Afterward, I pursued an M.S. in Computer Science at UT Austin, with the intention of moving into a data science-related role as my next endeavor. I am dedicated to becoming an ethical, effective programmer building technology that helps people.

Apart from coding, I am an avid fan of basketball and tennis, and I can often be found hitting on the tennis courts. I also enjoy listening to J-pop and watching anime in my free time.
