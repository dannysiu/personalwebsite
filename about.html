West Coast Education, Texas Roots About My Journey

I'm a senior at UC Berkeley studying data science who was raised in Texas. As a student, I choose to explore data science as it relates to the intersection of linguistics and computer science. I am dedicated to being an ethical programmer and building technology that helps people.

In the past, I have had the honor to conduct research in university labs. At UT Austin, I worked as a research assistant to computationally simulate chemical reactions. Later on, in my sophomore year at UC Berkeley, I helped to create machine learning models for climate chemistry datasets with the Cohen Research Group.

I have a profound interest in languages. Apart from Python and Java, I am currently working to become fluent in Japanese and improve my Cantonese, Mandarin, and Latin.

In my free time, I enjoy making music on the piano, watching anime, and playing tennis with friends.
